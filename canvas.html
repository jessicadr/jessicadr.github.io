CANVAS

How might we help students keep track of their courses in a simple and intuitive way?


Canvas is UBCâ€™s main academic platform where students, professors and teaching assistants can share assignments, discussions, announcements and grades with students as well as communicate over a messaging feature.

GOAL

Redesign the Canvas phone app to make its features more intuitive and useful to UBC students.

RESEARCH

USABILITY TESTING:

First, we conducted a pre-screen questionnaire to obtain some background info from our participants.
We then conducted 2 task scenarios with 20 students to identify the pain-points and potential improvements that could be made for the Canvas app. These tests were completed in person, and applied the think-aloud procedure.
Lastly, we asked 5 follow-up questions to expand more upon which specific features of the Canvas app work well for students, and if not, why.

4 PAIN POINTS

  1. Limited to-do list: only showed tasks for upcoming week.
  2. Unintuitive icons: crowded the interface and students had difficulty understanding their meanings.
  3. Indirect syllabus button: location of syllabus button was inconsistent between course pages and took multiple steps in order to be reached.
  4. Inconsistent email format: unfamiliar email layout required user to learn how to navigate email differently from how they would navigate their typical mail app, while also lacking functions such as viewing sent messages.

CONSTRAINTS

We acknowledge that by having a small sample size our data cannot be generalized to the whole UBC community.

SOLUTIONS

  • Updated to-do list to display all upcoming events in chronological order, with option to view events for all courses or for one course at a time.
  • Simplified icons and removed icons from crowded pages.
  • Created a consistent layout for course page with button that goes directly to syllabus PDF.
  • Updated email format to become more familiar/intuitive for the user and created option to view emails for all courses or a specific course.
