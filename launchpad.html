UBC Launch Pad

How might users keep track of and use their food before it expires?


Product: iOS app
Team: Connor Fong, Alan Yang, Wren Liang, Noor Khan, Nanda Syahrasyad, Andrew Zulaybar, Sang Le, Sandy Co, Jessica del Rosario
Role: UX/UI Designer
Tools: Sketch, InVision, Figma

Background

UBC Launchpad is a student-run software engineering team devoted to building software projects in a collaborative, professional environment. I am collaborating with a team of 7 developers, a strategist, and another UX/UI designer on this project. As UX/UI designers, we are currently conducting user research/user tests in addition to producing lo-fi, mid-fi, and hi-fi designs for the app.

Project Goal

Build, design, and conduct research for an iOS app that will allow users to track the food in their homes, see when items are expiring, and find recipes based on their inventory. Our team is currently aiming to have a finished product ready for launch by April 30, 2020, and are nearing the completion of our minimum viable product.

Research Process

Lofi:

  • Survey (78 participants)
  • User Interview (20 participants)

Midfi:

  • Think-aloud process
  • A/B testing
  • Further testing ongoing

Hifi:

  • Testing ongoing

Design Process

  1. Empathize: User interviews/surveys, seeking to understand
  2. Define: Pain points, prototyping personas, role objectives, challenges
  3. Ideate: Share/diverge/converge ideas, determine MVP
  4. Prototype: Mockups, simple storyboards, failing fast and iterating quickly
  5. Test: Understand problems, 'What works? What doesn't work?, iterate

Lofi Designs

After synthesizing the data from our user surveys and research, we narrowed down which features may be most useful and sketched out different iterations of wireframes for our MVP.


Midfi Designs

After completing more user tests and validating our lofi wireframes, we were able to design multiple versions of midfi wireframes, which we also then tested to narrow down the most intuitive iterations.


UI Library + Hifi Designs (in progress)

We were able to create a UI library featuring the main colour scheme and font we will be using. We are also currently in the process of converting our midfi designs to hifi, and are conducting some user tests in between this conversion in order to validate our designs.



A finished product will be available by April 30, 2020. I will continue to update the progress here until then!
